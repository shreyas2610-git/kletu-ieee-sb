Tech talk 14 Jun 2026

AI in Robotics — a tech talk

A session on how machine-learning models are reshaping real-world robotics, from perception stacks to imitation learning — with a live Q&A from industry.

When Saturday, 14 Jun 2026 · 2:00 PM
Where CSE Seminar Hall, KLE Tech
Audience Open to all engineering students

Why this talk

Robotics has quietly turned into one of the most applied ML problems in the field. Between foundation models for perception and reinforcement learning pipelines for manipulation, the landscape changes every quarter. This talk is a practical tour for students who want to get past the hype and understand what's actually shipping.

What we'll cover

  • Perception stacks — from classical SLAM to transformer-based world models.
  • Learning from demonstration: imitation learning and diffusion policies.
  • Sim-to-real: why it fails, what makes it work, and current benchmarks.
  • Career pathways for students interested in robotics research and industry roles.
If you've built anything in Gazebo or ROS, this will connect the dots between your bench project and what companies are shipping to factory floors today.

Who's speaking

A guest speaker from an industry robotics lab will open the session, followed by a moderated panel with faculty from the KLE Tech CSE and ECE departments. The full speaker list will be confirmed closer to the date.

How to join

Entry is free for all KLE Tech students and open to external attendees on a first-come basis. Registration opens two weeks before the event — watch the @ieee_kletu handle for the link.
